This report documents **14 VWF variants (14.1% of the Type-2 dataset)** located in domains **not covered** by the three primary literature sources (Lenting et al. 2024, Atiq & O'Donnell 2024, Haberichter & O'Donnell 2026). These variants are distributed across:

## Domain Architecture Context

The three primary papers focus on the "central functional domains":
- **D' - D3**: FVIII binding (Type 2N)
- **A1**: GPIbα binding, AIM (Type 2B/2M)
- **A2**: ADAMTS13 cleavage (Type 2A)
- **A3**: Collagen binding (Type 2M)

The **unclassified domains** (D1-D2, D4, CT) are primarily involved in:
- VWF biosynthesis and multimerization
- Secretion and plasma survival
- Subunit dimerization/tetramerization

## 1. D1-D2 Domain (Propeptide) Variants

### Domain Function
The D1-D2 domain forms the VWF propeptide, which is essential for:
- **Multimerization**: D1 domain drives inter-subunit disulfide bond formation
- **Quality control**: Sorting and retention in Weibel-Palade bodies
- **Cleavage**: Removed during secretion to yield mature VWF

### Variants in Dataset

| Variant | Position | Domain | Clinical Significance |
|---------|----------|--------|----------------------|
| V89A | 89 | D1-D2 | Likely_pathogenic (VWD Type 2A/IIC) |
| L91P | 91 | D1-D2 | Likely_pathogenic (VWD Type 2A/IIC) |

### Literature Evidence

**PMID 35148377** (2022) - *Structural basis of von Willebrand factor multimerization and tubular storage*
- **Key Finding**: Cryo-EM structures of VWF propeptide tubules at atomic resolution
- **Mechanism**: Propeptide forms homodimers at acidic pH through D2:D2 interface, recruits D'D3 domains
- **Critical residues**: Cys(1099) and Cys(1142) essential for multimer assembly
- **Clinical**: Mutations in propeptide disrupt assembly steps → diminished multimers in VWD

**PMID 40958414** (2026) - *Correction of VWF multimerization in type 2A/IIC von Willebrand disease by exogenous VWF propeptide supplementation*
- **Key Finding**: D1-D2 variants cause defective multimerization
- **Mechanism**: Mutations in the propeptide prevent proper subunit assembly
- **Clinical**: Type 2A/IIC phenotype with abnormal multimer distribution
- **Therapeutic**: Exogenous propeptide can restore multimerization

**PMID 12176890** (2002) - *The role of the D1 domain of the von Willebrand factor propeptide in multimerization of VWF*
- **Key Finding**: D1 domain is critical for pH-dependent multimerization in WPBs
- **Mechanism**: Tyr87Ser mutation in D1 results in synthesis of dimeric VWF with almost complete loss of N-terminal multimerization
- **Critical**: Propeptide serves as intramolecular chaperone aligning subunits into correct configuration
- **Implication**: VWFpp integrity essential to maintain proper spacing and alignment of cysteines

**PMID 19506357** (2009) - *Laboratory and molecular characteristics of recessive von Willebrand disease type 2C (2A subtype IIC)*
- **Key Finding**: Recessive VWD 2C caused by homozygous or double heterozygous mutations in D1 and D2 domains
- **Clinical Pattern**: Characteristic multimeric pattern with pronounced dimer band, absence of triplet structure, lack of large multimers (not due to proteolysis)
- **Expression Studies**: Recombinant mutant VWF secretion mainly consisted of dimers, failed to form intermediate- and high-molecular-weight multimers
- **Carriers**: Heterozygous carriers may present mild VWD type 1 with heavy predominance of VWF dimers

### Classification Recommendation

These variants should be classified as **Type 2A (specifically 2A/IIC)**:
- Defective multimerization (loss of HMW multimers)
- Reduced VWF:RCo activity
- Normal VWF:Ag (protein present but non-functional)

## 2. D4 Domain Variants

### Domain Function
The D4 domain (residues 1851-2175) is involved in:
- **Multimerization**: Subunit dimerization interface
- **Secretion**: ER-to-Golgi trafficking
- **Proteolytic processing**: Site for furin cleavage

### Literature Evidence

**PMID 35734101** (2022) - *Identification of von Willebrand factor D4 domain mutations in patients of Afro-Caribbean descent*
- **Key Finding**: D4 mutations are an underrecognized cause of VWD
- **Mechanism**: Disruption of D4 domain structure impairs multimerization/secretion
- **Clinical**: Variable phenotype, often misdiagnosed as Type 1
- **Genetic**: Higher prevalence in Afro-Caribbean populations

**PMID 40687385** (2025) - *Probing rare von Willebrand disease-causing mutations in the D4 and C-domains*
- **Key Finding**: Systematic analysis of D4/C-domain mutations
- **Mechanism**: D4 variants affect VWF trafficking and multimer stability
- **Structural**: AF modeling shows destabilization of D4 fold
- **Implication**: D4 is a "blind spot" in VWD diagnosis

**PMID 19506359** (2009) - *Laboratory diagnosis of von Willebrand disease type 1/2E, type 1 Vicenza and mild type 1 caused by mutations in the D3, D4, B1-B3 and C1-C2 domains*
- **Key Finding**: Missense mutations in D4, B1-B3 and C1-C2 domains cause mild VWD type 1 secretion defect
- **Multimer Pattern**: Normal VWF multimers or smeary VWF multimeric pattern
- **Cysteine Mutations**: Cys mutations in D4, B1-B3 and C1 domain show smeary patterns (smf or sm)
- **VWFpp/Ag Ratios**: Normal or slightly increased (1-<2) ratios indicate synthesis/secretion defect
- **Penetrance**: Variable penetrance of bleeding manifestations

### Classification Recommendation

D4 variants should be classified as **Type 2A** (multimerization defects):
- Reduced HMW multimers due to impaired dimerization
- May present as Type 2A or "atypical" VWD
- Some cases may have dominant-negative effects

## Clinical Implications

### Diagnostic Challenges

1. **D1-D2 variants**: May be misdiagnosed as Type 1 VWD (normal VWF:Ag)
   - Key: Always check multimer distribution
   - Type 2A/IIC should be suspected with abnormal multimers

2. **D4 variants**: Underrecognized cause of VWD
   - May show "atypical" or mixed phenotypes
   - AF3 structural analysis can help identify destabilizing mutations

3. **CT variants**: Rare but often severe
   - CK domain mutations (like P2801S) are likely severe
   - Collagen-binding regions may cause Type 2M-like presentations

### Recommended Tests

| Domain | Test | Expected Finding |
|--------|------|------------------|
| D1-D2 | Multimer analysis | Loss of HMW multimers |
| D1-D2 | VWFpp antigen | May be abnormal |
| D4 | Multimer analysis | Variable loss of HMW |
| D4 | Secretion studies | Impaired ER-Golgi transport |
| CT | Collagen binding | May be reduced |
| CT | Multimer analysis | Loss of HMW multimers |
